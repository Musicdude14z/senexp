History

  • Magnetic fusion research at Princeton began in 1951
    • Inspired by the research of Professor Spitzer
  • The project was soon changed to PPPL in 1961

Statistics & Facts

- 2012
  • PPPL is funded by the U.S. Department of Energy (DoE) through the Office of Science
  • 454 full time employees
    • Not counting 38 graduate students, additional subcontractors, and visiting research staff

Current Info

Mission Statement

"The DOE Princeton Plasma Physics Laboratory works with collaborators across the globe to develop fusion as an energy source for the world, and conducts research along the broad frontier of plasma science and technology. PPPL also nurtures the national research enterprise in these fields, and educates the next generation of plasma and fusion scientists."

Vision

"Enabling a world powered by safe, clean and plentiful fusion energy while leading discoveries in plasma science and technology."

The Essentials

  • General background in Computer Science
  • Ideally familiar with Python
    • If not, at least some other C-style language (eg. C++, Java, JavaScript, Ruby)
  • Comfortable using online documentation to understand an unfamiliar API
  • Basic concept of a POSIX shell and a unix/linux os
    • However, there are Windows and Macintosh computers available to work on
  • Interest in Visualization and Solving Problems with Computer Science

The Less-Essentials

Beneficial Qualifications that are Not Necessary to Start this Internship
  • Familiarity using a linux based operating system, and using the shell
  • Familiarity using ssh and scp to connect to and interact with a remote host
  • Comfortable using visualization software such as VisIt or Paraview
  • Access to a POSIX terminal at home, to do work through over a VPN when offsite
    • This is not necessary, as there are other ways to work remotely over the VPN

Skeleton of Multiprocessing Code 

#!/usr/bin/python
from multiprocessing import Pool
#define routine BEFORE creating pool
def routine(*args, **kwargs):
	pass
#define callback (if wanted) BEFORE creating pool
def done(ret_val):
	pass
#create pool
p = Pool()
#Then read in large data AFTER creating pool
data = get_data()
#Schedule functions to evaluate with pool
for args in data:
	p.apply_async(routine, args, callback=done)
#Tell pool you are done scheduling functions
p.close()
#Tell pool to wait in main process 
#until all child processes terminate
p.join()
